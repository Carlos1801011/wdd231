How Do You Make a Sourdough Starter?

Creating a new sourdough starter is like starting a friendship. It takes a little patience and some good vibes. Here's a quick recipe for making your own:

  1. Mix: Combine equal parts (by weight) of whole grain rye flour and water. Stir it well‚Äîencourage those little yeasties to mingle.
  2. Feed: Every day, discard some of the mixture (don't worry, it's normal!) and add fresh flour and water. This keeps your starter well-fed and happy.

Your First Recipe!

    Ingredients:
  • - 500 grams of wheat flour
  • - 350 milliliters of warm water
  • - 100 grams of active sourdough starter
  • - 10 grams of salt
  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour with the warm water and the sourdough starter. Stir until all ingredients are well combined.
  2. Add the salt and mix again until well incorporated.
  3. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
  4. After resting, knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover again and let it ferment in a warm place for 4 to 6 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  6. Once it has fermented, take the dough out of the bowl and place it on a floured surface. Shape it into a loaf and place it in a proofing basket.
  7. Let it rest for another 1 to 2 hours.
  8. Preheat the oven to 230 degrees Celsius. Place a cast iron pot inside to heat up.
  9. When the oven is ready, carefully take out the pot, place the dough inside, and cover it.
  10. Bake for 30 minutes, then uncover and bake for another 15 minutes to brown the crust.
  11. Enjoy your baking!
